    • REDBREAST

    Type: Single Pot Still Irish Whiskey

    Description: Redbreast is a well-regarded brand of Irish whiskey known for producing single pot still expressions. Single pot still whiskey is a traditional style unique to Ireland, crafted using a mix of malted and unmalted barley and distilled in pot stills. Redbreast, produced by the Midleton Distillery, has gained a reputation for its rich and flavorful character.

    Flavor Profile: Redbreast whiskies are characterized by a full-bodied and complex flavor profile. Common tasting notes include a combination of fruity, spicy, and nutty elements. The influence of sherry cask aging is often evident, imparting sweetness, dried fruit, and oak notes to the whiskey.

    Types of Redbreast Whiskey: Redbreast 12-Year-Old: The flagship expression, aged for 12 years, offering a balance of fruitiness, spices, and a creamy mouthfeel. Redbreast 15-Year-Old: Aged for a longer period, this expression adds depth and complexity to the flavor profile, with richer sherry influences. Redbreast 21-Year-Old: A more mature and limited edition, showcasing extended aging and a refined interplay of flavors. Aging Process: Redbreast whiskies are typically aged in a combination of American oak and sherry casks. The use of sherry casks contributes to the rich and fruity characteristics, while American oak imparts vanilla and oak notes.

    Color: The color of Redbreast whiskies varies across expressions, ranging from golden amber to deep mahogany, influenced by the aging process and cask types.

    Awards: Redbreast whiskies have received numerous awards and accolades for their quality and craftsmanship, solidifying their standing among the finest Irish whiskies.

    Legacy: Redbreast has a history dating back to the early 20th century, and its legacy is intertwined with the tradition of single pot still Irish whiskey production. It has become a brand that whiskey enthusiasts often associate with quality and heritage.

    How to Enjoy: Redbreast whiskies are often enjoyed neat or with a splash of water to unlock their full range of flavors. The complexity and depth of these whiskies make them suitable for sipping and savoring, allowing enthusiasts to appreciate the nuances of each expression.
